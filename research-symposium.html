The Science Automation Technologies Research Group (SciTech) at the USC Information Sciences Institute aims to empower the scientific community by tightening the relations between the domain scientists and current computational resources. As a result, scientists can focus on their research questions, while our open-source tools provide the computational foundations to seamlessly run their experiments and analyses in local and distributed resources. Our areas of focus are Workflow Management Systems, Enhancing National Cyberinfrastructure, Machine Learning, and Modeling and Simulation of Distributed Computing Systems.

In the past academic year, SciTech has included a number of MS and undergraduate students and we wanted to provide a forum for them to publicize the work they have been doing. Therefore, SciTech has partnered with Texas Tech University and the University of Notre Dame to hold a forum for students where scholarly and creative work by students will be showcased to the community called the SciTech & Friends Research Symposium.
